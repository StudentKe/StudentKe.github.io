Penguin chicks rescued by unlikely hero
By Tom Aglietti / bbc
Underestimating a penguin is a mistake that might cost you dearly
Penguins have an undeserved reputation as funny and somewhat ridiculous animals and it’s easy to see why: they’re cute, flightless birds that waddle about like fluffy, drunk toddlers. Hollywood casts Danny Devito as your human equivalent for a reason.
However, underestimating them is a mistake that might cost you dearly, as one petrel bird discovered when it attacked a group of emperor penguin chicks hoping for an easy meal.
As these chicks made their way to the sea without their parents’ supervision for the first time, a predatory giant petrel swooped in for what it thought would prove a quick kill. The chicks initially tried to flee, but struggled with running on land, and, in a case of ironically unhappy feet, one of them slipped and fell, becoming an easy prey to the predatory bird.
When the petrel grabbed him by the neck with its strong beak, it looked like there was little or no hope left for the clumsy chick, but a tactical fall allowed it to escape and slide away back to its group.
Rejoined by their fallen companion, the chicks assembled into a defensive circle that allowed them to protect each other from all sides. The hunt was brought to a stall. After a tense stand off, the arrival of an adult adelie, a smaller, but extremely feisty species of penguin, finally set the petrel on the run.
Predators like the petrel are just one of the many dangers encountered daily by penguins.
What strategies do they adopt to survive in one of the most inhospitable environment in the world?
STRENGTH IN NUMBERS
Strength in numbers is the most known survival strategy for penguins. Huddling together allows them to find shelter from the extreme cold and unforgiving snow storms, but also functions as protection to fight off some of the many, deadly predators that inhabit Antarctica.
COUNTERSHADING
Disguise is another fundamental skill for the survival of penguins. Their typical black and white coat is more than just a fabulous look: the colour arrangement provides penguins with counter-shading, a method of camouflage used by many animals where their top colour is much darker than their underside. In the case of penguins, it allows them to stay hidden from both predators and preys when in the sea: their white belly blends them with the bright sea surface, while the black feathers make them disappear into the dark bottom of the ocean. Fashionable AND practical!
TOBOGGANING
Penguin bellies are not just useful for camouflage: when on land, they are also the tools that provide them with their fastest escape technique, tobogganing, the word that better describes when penguins slide on their belly to gain speed on ice. Curiously, Penguins have been observed tobogganing not only to escape predators or move faster, but also just for fun.
JUMPING
There’s another, amazing technique Penguins use to escape predators, and that’s jumping out of water up to nine feet straight. For small, flightless birds, that seems like an incredible feat, and Penguins achieve it by trapping air bubbles under their plumage before diving. When they then release these air-bubbles as they swim towards the surface, the air coats their bodies like a lubricant, allowing them to achieve the great speed that lets them go airborne once outside the water.

Cutting carbohydrates (碳水化合物) might also cut lifespan by up to four years, according to a new medical study.

减少碳水化合物(碳水化合物)也可能使寿命减少到四年,根据一项新的医学研究。
The peer-reviewed research published in the medical journal “The Lancet Public Health”(《柳叶刀》的公共卫生) suggests low and high-carb diets could shorten life, and diets including some carbs could promote a healthy lifespan

同行评议的研究发表在医学杂志《柳叶刀》“公共卫生”(《柳叶刀》的公共卫生)表明,低和高碳水化合物饮食可能会缩短人们的寿命,和饮食包括一些碳水化合物能促进健康的寿命。
The study analyzed self-reported data from more than 15,400 middle-aged Americans who participated in the Atherosclerosis Risk in Communities Study(动脉粥样硬化的风险在社区研究). The dietary patterns(饮食模式)researchers found were compared against additional studies that included 432,000 people in more than 20 countries.

研究分析了自我报告的数据来自15400多个社区中年美国人参与了动脉粥样硬化风险研究(动脉粥样硬化的风险在社区研究)。饮食模式(饮食模式)研究人员发现比较反对额外的研究,其中包括432000人在20多个国家。
Researchers concluded that people who ate a moderate amount of carbohydrates lived four years longer than those with low-carbohydrate consumption and one year longer than those who ate a lot of carbohydrates.
研究人员得出结论，摄入适量碳水化合物的人比摄入少量碳水化合物的人多活4年，比摄入大量碳水化合物的人多活1年。
Low-carb diets were defined as less than 40 percent of calories from carbohydrates and high-carb diets were more than 70 percent of calories.

低碳水化合物饮食的定义是碳水化合物摄入的热量低于40%，而高碳水化合物饮食的热量高于70%。
“Our data suggests that animal-based low carbohydrate diets, which are prevalent in North America and Europe, might be associated with shorter overall life span and should be discouraged.”

“我们的数据表明，北美和欧洲盛行的以动物为基础的低碳水化合物饮食可能与较短的总体寿命有关，应该予以劝阻。”
Researchers observed that people who replaced carbohydrates with protein and fat (蛋白质和脂肪) from animals had a higher risk of early death compared to those who replaced carbohydrates with plant-based foods

研究人员观察到的人取代碳水化合物与蛋白质和脂肪(蛋白质和脂肪)从动物早死的风险更高比起那些取代碳水化合物和植物性食物。

“These findings bring together several strands that have been controversial(有争议的). Too much and too little carbohydrate can be harmful but what counts most is the type of fat, protein, and carbohydrate.”
“这些发现召集几股一直存有争议(有争议的)。碳水化合物过多或过少都是有害的，但最重要的是脂肪、蛋白质和碳水化合物的类型。
Self-reported data can be flawed, because it relies on the subject’s memory. Another limitation of the study: Diets were measured only twice during the 25-year study period, at the start of the study and again six years later.

自我报告的数据可能有缺陷，因为它依赖于受试者的记忆。这项研究的另一个局限性是:在25年的研究期间，饮食只被测量了两次，一次是在研究开始时，另一次是在6年后。

12种不能给狗吃的食物
Avocado(鳄梨)
These fleshy(肉) green fruits contain(包含) a fungicidal(真菌) toxin(毒素) . called persin(专业名词) which could give your dog a digestive(消化) upset. it’s the large avocado stone that presents most danger because it could potentially(潜在的) block the gastrointestinal tract(肠道管). , leaving your dog requiring surgery(手术).
Bones（骨头）
Contrary(相反的) to popular opinion, bones are bad for dogs. Not only can they potentially choke(窒息) your dog, they can cause blockages (堵塞物)in the gut（胆脏）, intestinal(肠道) damage and chipped(缺口) or broken teeth.
Bread dough(面包面团)
The smell(气味) of unbaked(未烘焙的) yeasty(酵母) bread dough could attract your dog’s attention but it should always be kept out of reach(延伸). If eaten, the dough could expand dangerously in your dog’s stomach, and the fermented(发酵) yeast could become toxic.
Chocolate
Chocolate and cocoa-based products contain(包含) a stimulating(兴奋地，启发的)substance called theobromine(可可碱) which is extremely poisonous(恶毒的) to dogs – the darker the chocolate, the greater the risk. Even a few ounces (一盎司) of chocolate could leave your dog with sickness, diarrhea(复写), dehydration(脱水), and high blood pressure, and the effect is potentially worse in small breeds(品种). If your dog appears restless or agitated (不安) and you think chocolate poisoning(中毒) could be the cause, contact your vet(兽医).
Caffeine
Any product with caffeine can have a similar effect on your dog to chocolate and could ultimately damage its heart. So, keep your coffee, teabags and fizzy(气泡的) drinks well away from inquisitive(好奇的) tongues(舌头).
Dried fruit(干果)
Raisins , currants and sultanas (三种葡萄干) affect different dogs in different ways and scientists can’t work out why. However, experts agree that they contain(包含) chemical compounds(混合物) that are potentially very toxic to dogs. At best, your dog could suffer from diarrhea(腹泻)and vomiting(吐 and, at worst, dried-fruit poisoning could lead to kidney(肾) failure.
Fresh fruit
Like dried fruit, grapes are highly toxic and should be kept away from your pet. The pips and stones of some fruits (such as apples, plums, peaches and cherries) contain a chemical called amygdalin(扁桃甙), which can cause a digestive upset. Large fruit stones are also a choking hazard - particularly among puppies and small breeds(小狗和小品种。).
Garlic and onion(这段生词太多了，我翻译注在下面了)
Members of the allium family (including garlic, leeks and onions) contain a substance called organosulfides which could affect your dog’s red blood cells – too much garlic or onion (either raw or cooked) could ultimately lead to anaemia. The symptoms take a few days to appear so if your dog appears sleepy and lethargic , head straight to your vet.
大蒜和洋葱
葱属植物的成员（包括大蒜，韭菜和洋葱）含有一种叫做有机硫化物的物质，可能会影响你的狗的红细胞 - 过多的大蒜或洋葱（生的或煮熟的）最终会导致贫血。症状需要几天时间才会出现，所以如果你的狗出现困倦和昏昏欲睡，请直接前往兽医。
Nuts
Nuts are high in fat and should be avoided by dogs but the most toxic of all are macadamia(澳洲) nuts. They can cause vomiting, increased temperature（体温升高）, tremorsand(震颤)a rapid heart beat(心跳加快).
Xylitol(这段生词太多了，我翻译注在下面了)
This artificial sweetener is used in sweets, cakes, toothpaste and chewing gum and has a laxative effect on humans. For dogs, though, an excess of xylitol could prove fatal because it kickstarts a rapid release of the hormone insulin. Too much insulin affects the amount of glucose (sugar) in your dog’s blood which, in turn, could cause seizures and affect your pet’s liver. The symptoms of xylitol poisoning include weakness, vomiting and a lack of co-ordination, and it requires urgent veterinary attention.
木糖醇
这种人造甜味剂用于糖果，蛋糕，牙膏和口香糖中，对人类具有通便作用。然而，对于狗来说，过量的木糖醇可能会致命，因为它可以迅速释放激素胰岛素。过多的胰岛素会影响狗血液中的葡萄糖（糖），反过来，这会导致癫痫发作并影响宠物的肝脏。木糖醇中毒的症状包括虚弱，呕吐和缺乏协调，需要紧急兽医注意。
